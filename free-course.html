What is Trading?

Trading is the act of buying and selling financial instruments (like currencies, stocks, indices, or cryptocurrencies) with the goal of making a profit from price movements.

Key point: You are speculating on price direction, not owning the asset long-term like investing.

2

Trading Platforms & Tools

The most popular free platform is TradingView. It allows you to view charts, draw trendlines, use indicators, and analyze any market.

📊 TradingView

3

Candlestick Charts

Each candle represents price movement during a specific time period (1 minute, 5 minutes, 1 hour, etc.).

Green candle = price went up
Red candle = price went down

4

Market Structure

Market structure is the pattern of higher highs, higher lows (uptrend) or lower highs, lower lows (downtrend).

5

Fair Value Gap (FVG)

A Fair Value Gap is a price area where the market moved very quickly, leaving an inefficiency (gap) that price often returns to fill later.

📍 FVG

A zone the market "skipped" and tends to revisit.

6

Basic Risk Management

Never risk more than 1% of your account on a single trade. This is the golden rule of professional traders.

1% Rule
