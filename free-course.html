What is Trading?

Trading is the act of buying and selling financial instruments (Forex, stocks, crypto, indices, commodities) with the goal of making a profit from changes in price.

Unlike investing, which is long-term (years), trading is usually short to medium term — from minutes to several weeks.

2

Main Financial Markets

The most commonly traded markets for retail traders:

  • Forex (Foreign Exchange) – The largest market in the world. You trade currency pairs like EURUSD, GBPJPY.
  • Stock Indices – Represent baskets of stocks (S&P 500, NASDAQ, DAX, Nikkei).
  • Cryptocurrencies – Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, etc. Highly volatile.
  • Commodities – Gold, Silver, Crude Oil, Natural Gas.

4

Order Types You Must Know

  • Market Order – Executes immediately at the current market price.
  • Limit Order – Executes only at a specific price or better.
  • Stop Loss (SL) – Automatically closes your trade to limit losses.
  • Take Profit (TP) – Automatically closes your trade when your target is reached.
5

Candlestick Charts

Each candle represents price movement during a chosen time period (1 min, 5 min, 1 hour, daily, etc.).

Green candle = bullish (close > open)
Red candle = bearish (close < open)

The body shows open and close price. The wicks show the highest and lowest price during that period.

6

Market Structure & Trends

Market structure refers to the overall direction of price movement:

  • Uptrend: Higher highs and higher lows
  • Downtrend: Lower highs and lower lows
  • Sideways / Ranging: Price moves between clear support and resistance levels
7

Support and Resistance

Support is a price level where buying pressure is strong enough to prevent the price from falling further.

Resistance is a price level where selling pressure is strong enough to prevent the price from rising further.

These levels are watched by thousands of traders and often act as reversal or continuation points.

8

Fibonacci Retracement

Fibonacci retracement is a tool used to identify potential support and resistance levels during a pullback in a trend.

The most important levels are: 23.6%, 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%, 78.6%.

Traders use these levels to find good entry points when the price corrects.

9

Moving Averages

Moving Averages smooth out price data to help identify the trend direction and dynamic support/resistance.

  • Simple Moving Average (SMA)
  • Exponential Moving Average (EMA) – gives more weight to recent prices

Popular periods: 9, 21, 50, 100, 200.

10

Volume – Why It Matters

Volume shows how many units were traded during a given period. It confirms the strength of a move.

  • High volume + strong price move = strong conviction
  • Low volume + price move = weak move, likely to reverse
  • Volume often increases at important support/resistance levels
11

Trading Sessions & Market Times

The forex market is open 24 hours a day, 5 days a week. There are 4 major trading sessions:

  • London Session – Highest liquidity and volatility (8:00 – 17:00 GMT)
  • New York Session – Overlaps with London, very active (13:00 – 22:00 GMT)
  • Asian Session – Usually calmer (00:00 – 09:00 GMT)
  • Sydney Session

The London-New York overlap is generally the most volatile period.

12

Risk Management & Trading Psychology

This is the foundation of long-term success in trading.

  • Never risk more than 1% of your total account on a single trade
  • Always use a stop loss
  • Keep a detailed trading journal
  • Control your emotions – fear, greed, revenge trading, and FOMO are the biggest account killers
  • Trading is 80% psychology and only 20% strategy
