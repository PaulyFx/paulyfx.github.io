What is Trading?

Trading is the process of buying and selling financial assets (currencies, stocks, cryptocurrencies, indices, commodities) with the goal of making a profit from price changes.

Important difference: Trading is short-term (minutes to weeks), while investing is long-term (years).

2

How Do Financial Markets Work?

Markets are places where buyers and sellers meet. Price moves based on supply and demand.

  • When more people want to buy → price goes up
  • When more people want to sell → price goes down
  • News, economic data, fear and greed all influence price movement
3

Main Trading Markets

The most popular markets for beginners:

  • Forex – Currency pairs (EURUSD, GBPJPY…)
  • Indices – Stock market baskets (S&P 500, NAS100, DAX)
  • Cryptocurrencies – Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana
  • Commodities – Gold, Oil, Natural Gas

5

Candlestick Charts

Each candle shows the price movement during a chosen time period (1 minute, 15 minutes, 1 hour, etc.).

Green candle = price closed higher than it opened
Red candle = price closed lower than it opened

The body shows the open and close price. The wicks (shadows) show the highest and lowest price during that period.

6

Market Structure & Trends

Market structure is the overall direction of the price.

  • Uptrend: Higher highs and higher lows
  • Downtrend: Lower highs and lower lows
  • Sideways / Ranging: Price moves in a horizontal range

Understanding the current market structure is one of the most important skills in trading.

7

Support and Resistance

Support = a price level where the price tends to stop falling and bounce up.
Resistance = a price level where the price tends to stop rising and fall back down.

These levels are created by previous price reactions and are watched by thousands of traders.

8

Basic Risk Management & Psychology

This is the most important part of trading.

  • Never risk more than 1% of your account on a single trade
  • Always use a stop loss
  • Trading is 80% psychology and only 20% strategy
  • Emotional control (fear, greed, revenge) is what separates winners from losers
