What is Trading?

Trading is the act of buying and selling financial instruments (Forex, stocks, crypto, indices, commodities) with the goal of profiting from price movements.

Key difference from investing: Trading is usually short-term (minutes to weeks), while investing is long-term (years).

2

Main Financial Markets

The most popular markets for retail traders:

  • Forex – Currency pairs (EURUSD, GBPJPY, etc.) – largest and most liquid market
  • Stock Indices – S&P 500, NASDAQ, DAX, Nikkei
  • Cryptocurrencies – Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana
  • Commodities – Gold, Silver, Crude Oil
3

Trading Platforms & Tools

The best free platform is TradingView. It is used by millions of traders worldwide.

Other important tools:

  • Broker (where you actually open trades)
  • Economic calendar (news events)
  • Screeners and watchlists
4

Order Types You Must Know

  • Market Order – Execute immediately at current price
  • Limit Order – Buy/sell only at a specific price or better
  • Stop Loss – Automatically closes your trade if price goes against you
  • Take Profit – Automatically closes your trade when target is reached
5

Candlestick Charts Explained

Each candle represents price action during a chosen timeframe.

Green/White candle = bullish (close higher than open)
Red/Black candle = bearish (close lower than open)

The body shows open and close. The wicks show the highest and lowest price during that period.

6

Market Structure & Trends

Market structure is the "big picture" direction of price:

  • Uptrend: Series of higher highs and higher lows
  • Downtrend: Series of lower highs and lower lows
  • Sideways/Ranging: Price moves between support and resistance
7

Support & Resistance

Support = price level where buying interest is strong enough to stop the price from falling further.
Resistance = price level where selling interest is strong enough to stop the price from rising further.

These levels are watched by thousands of traders and often act as turning points.

8

Fibonacci Retracement

Fibonacci is a popular tool used to find potential support and resistance levels during pullbacks.

The most important levels are: 23.6%, 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%, 78.6%

Traders use Fibonacci to find good entry points during corrections in a trend.

9

Moving Averages

Moving Averages smooth out price action and help identify trend direction.

  • Simple Moving Average (SMA)
  • Exponential Moving Average (EMA) – more responsive to recent price

Common combinations: 50 & 200 period MA (Golden/Death Cross).

10

Risk Management & Trading Psychology

This is the most important lesson.

  • Never risk more than 1% of your account on a single trade
  • Always use stop loss
  • Keep a trading journal
  • Control your emotions (fear, greed, revenge trading)
  • Trading is 80% psychology, 20% strategy
